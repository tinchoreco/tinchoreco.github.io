The Complete Map

How the pieces fit together

Throughout this series we've traveled from how two agents decide to cooperate to why the global economy is fundamentally unpredictable. Here's how the pieces fit.

The Causal Chain

c₁ < 0 → Concave potential → Bistability + Logic gates → Turing completeness

Turing completeness → H(n) incomputable → Ωₙ₊₁ undecidable → Supercomputational growth

Each arrow is a formally proven theorem.

The Division of Labor

Combining knowledge across k individuals with complexity n₀ gives access to technologies of complexity k·n₀, whose number exceeds k times the individual technologies by an unbounded factor:

k·H(n₀) ≪ H(k·n₀)

The central idea, in one line:

The economy is a computational system whose limits are the limits of computation itself.
