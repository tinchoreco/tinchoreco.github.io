Goods, Technologies, and Chaitin's Constant

The Turing-Walras-Chaitin framework

The TWC framework proposes three formal correspondences: Goods are bit strings. Technologies are Turing machines. The technological frontier is Ωₙ.

Two central results:

First, for each frontier level n, competitive equilibrium exists and is computable. But from Ωₙ, you cannot compute the equilibrium of Ωₙ₊₁. Why? Because knowing the next level's equilibrium requires knowing which new programs halt—exactly the halting problem, which is undecidable.

Second, growth is supercomputational. The number of viable technologies H(n) grows faster than any computable function. The practical implication: any long-term economic forecast necessarily underestimates growth.
