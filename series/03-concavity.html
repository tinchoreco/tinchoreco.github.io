What Is Concavity and Why Does It Matter?

The hidden assumption of all economic theory

Open any microeconomics textbook and you'll find an assumption on almost every page: concavity. Utility functions are concave. Production functions are concave. Why?

Our result provides a different explanation: concavity is assumed because without it, equilibrium analysis is impossible. Not difficult—impossible, in the rigorous mathematical sense.

We prove that in the regime c₁ < 0, the dynamics are Turing complete: they can implement any calculation, including undecidable ones. No algorithm can predict the system's long-term behavior. Changing the sign of a single parameter crosses the boundary between the computable and the incomputable.
