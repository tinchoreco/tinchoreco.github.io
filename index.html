The TWC Framework

01

Goods as Computation

Economic goods are finite binary sequences. Technologies are Turing machines that transform inputs into outputs. Production is computation.

02

Incomputable Frontier

Equilibrium at stage n is computable. But the transition to n+1 is not—each expansion requires new bits of Chaitin's algorithmically random Ω.

03

Supercomputational Growth

Output grows faster than any computable function. The Solow residual is the growth of Ωn—accumulated algorithmic knowledge.

04

Division of Labor

Combining knowledge across k agents yields supercomputational returns—formalizing Smith's insight as a theorem of algorithmic information theory.
